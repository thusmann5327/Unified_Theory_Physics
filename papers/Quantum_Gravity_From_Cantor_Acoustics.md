## 7. Physical Interpretation

### Why gravity is weak

Gravity requires a double-fold crossing — interference between two orthogonal Cantor lattice planes. Each bracket attenuates the signal by √(1−W²)/φ ≈ 0.55. Over 136 brackets (the structural midpoint 4 × F(9)), this compounds to 10⁻³⁶. Gravity is weak because it has to tunnel through 136 partially-opaque Cantor barriers using only the acoustic channels.

### Why the cosmological constant is small

Vacuum energy is the energy of the lattice itself. It decays by 1/φ per bracket — the bare golden ratio, without acoustic correction — because it doesn't propagate through the lattice; it IS the lattice. Over 588 brackets (the full Hubble span counted twice for two fold planes), this compounds to 10⁻¹²³. The cosmological constant is small because the lattice is big: 294 brackets from Planck to Hubble, and the energy density drops by φ at every level.

### Why electromagnetism is strong

The fine structure constant doesn't involve exponential attenuation. It's a COUNT: N brackets × W wall fraction = 137.3. Electromagnetic coupling measures the density of Cantor boundaries, not the transmission through them. That's why α is O(10⁻²) while gravity is O(10⁻³⁶) — counting walls is a linear operation; propagating through them is exponential.

### The hierarchy as a propagation problem

$$\alpha^{-1} \sim N \times W \quad (\text{linear: count walls})$$

$$\frac{G}{F_{EM}} \sim \left(\frac{\sqrt{1-W^2}}{\varphi}\right)^{136} \quad (\text{exponential: propagate acoustically})$$

$$\frac{\Lambda}{\Lambda_P} \sim \left(\frac{1}{\varphi}\right)^{588} \quad (\text{exponential: decay through lattice})$$

The hierarchy problem is not mysterious. It's the difference between counting and exponentiating. Linear operations (EM coupling) give O(100) numbers. Exponential operations over O(100) brackets give O(10⁻³⁶) and O(10⁻¹²²) numbers. The framework makes this distinction structural.
