## The Two Boundaries (Lineweaver & Patel)

Every object in the universe lives on a log(mass) vs log(radius) plot bounded by two lines:

**The Schwarzschild line** — gravity's maximum reach:

$$r_s = \frac{2Gm}{c^2} \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad \log r = \log m + \log\!\left(\frac{2G}{c^2}\right)$$

Slope +1 in log-log. Objects below this line are denser than black holes. All black holes sit ON this line. The observable universe (Hubble radius, total mass) also sits on it.

**The Compton line** — quantum's minimum size:

$$\lambda_c = \frac{\hbar}{mc} \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad \log r = -\log m + \log\!\left(\frac{\hbar}{c}\right)$$

Slope −1 in log-log. Objects below this line are more localized than quantum mechanics allows. All fundamental particles sit ON this line.

**The two lines are perpendicular** (slopes +1 and −1). They intersect at the Planck point:

$$m_P = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c}{G}} = 2.176 \times 10^{-8} \text{ kg} \qquad l_P = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^3}} = 1.616 \times 10^{-35} \text{ m}$$

All observable matter exists in the triangular region BETWEEN these two forbidden zones.

## The Gate Equation

Any object with mass m occupies a position on this plot. Its radius r must satisfy:

$$\frac{\hbar}{mc} \;\leq\; r \;\leq\; \frac{2Gm}{c^2}$$

The LEFT boundary (Compton) is the silver gate. The RIGHT boundary (Schwarzschild) is the gold gate. An object exists when it fits between the two gates. Rewrite this as a single dimensionless inequality by dividing all terms by the Planck length:

$$\frac{1}{\mu} \;\leq\; \rho \;\leq\; 2\mu$$

where μ = m/m_P (mass in Planck units) and ρ = r/l_P (radius in Planck units).

**The allowed region** is where both gates are satisfied simultaneously:

$$\rho^2 \;\leq\; 4\mu^2 \qquad \text{AND} \qquad \rho^2 \;\geq\; \frac{1}{\mu^2}$$

Multiply these two conditions:

$$\frac{1}{\mu^2} \;\leq\; \rho^2 \;\leq\; 4\mu^2$$

This is the complete Lineweaver-Patel triangle in one line. Every object in the universe satisfies this inequality.

## The Factor of 2: Two Fold Planes

Lineweaver and Patel note that the Schwarzschild radius of a Planck-mass black hole is r_s = 2l_P, not l_P. The lines don't quite cross at the Planck point — there's a factor of 2.

In the framework: **the factor of 2 counts fold planes.** The baryon mechanism requires matter to exist at the intersection of TWO orthogonal fold axes (the silver axis and gold axis). Each fold plane contributes one factor of Gm/c². Two planes give 2Gm/c² = r_s.

For a maximally rotating Kerr black hole, the inner (Cauchy) and outer horizons merge at r = r_s/2 = Gm/c². This is one fold plane — the dark matter configuration. Two fold planes (both axes confined) give the full Schwarzschild radius and baryonic matter.

$$r_s = 2 \times \frac{Gm}{c^2} = \underbrace{\frac{Gm}{c^2}}_{\text{silver fold}} + \underbrace{\frac{Gm}{c^2}}_{\text{gold fold}}$$
