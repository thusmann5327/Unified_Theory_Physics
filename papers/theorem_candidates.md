$$\frac{2}{\varphi^4} + \frac{3}{\varphi^3} = 1$$

Status: **THEOREM.** Proof: add 1/φ⁴ to both sides of T2, use T1. Physical interpretation: 2 endpoints + 3 interiors = 5 Cantor sectors.

$$\arctan\!\left(\frac{1}{\varphi}\right) + \arctan\!\left(\frac{1}{\varphi^3}\right) = \frac{\pi}{4}$$

Status: **THEOREM.** Proof: apply the tangent addition formula, simplify using T1. Verified to 10⁻¹⁶. Five independent routes from φ to π exist (pentagon, decagon, golden angle, Fibonacci cascade).

### T5. The Discriminant Fibonacci Chain

For metallic means δ_n satisfying x² = nx + 1, the discriminants Δ_n = n² + 4 form a Fibonacci chain:

$$\Delta_1 = 5 = F(5), \quad \Delta_2 = 8 = F(6), \quad \Delta_3 = 13 = F(7)$$

$$5 + 8 = 13 \qquad \text{but} \qquad 8 + 13 = 21 \neq 20 = \Delta_4$$

Status: **THEOREM.** Proof: Δ_{n-1} + Δ_n = Δ_{n+1} requires (n−2)² = 0, so n = 2 is the unique link. The chain holds if and only if n ≤ 3. ∎

### T6. The Discriminant Pythagorean

$$(\sqrt{5})^2 + (\sqrt{8})^2 = (\sqrt{13})^2$$

Status: **THEOREM.** Proof: immediate from 5 + 8 = 13. Physical interpretation: bronze (observable) = Pythagorean combination of gold (momentum) + silver (mass). Three dimensions is a theorem, not an assumption.

### T7. The Uniqueness of Three Dimensions

The discriminant chain breaks at n = 4 because Δ₄ = 20 ≠ 21 = F(8).

Status: **THEOREM.** Proof: part of T5. No fourth spatial dimension can be generated by the metallic mean hierarchy.

### T8. The √5 Exponent Identity

$$\varphi^2 \times r_c = \sqrt{5}$$

where r_c = 1 − 1/φ⁴ is the crossover parameter.

Status: **THEOREM.** Proof: φ²(1 − 1/φ⁴) = φ + 1 − 1/φ² − 1/φ³ = φ + 1/φ = √5. ∎ Physical interpretation: connects the non-interacting (φ²) and interacting (2/r_c) quantum Hall exponents.

### T9. The Observer Recursion

$$\frac{1}{\varphi^4} + \frac{1}{\varphi^5} = \frac{1}{\varphi^3}$$

Status: **THEOREM.** Proof: divide T1 by φ⁵. Physical interpretation: matter (1/φ⁴) + one recursion level (1/φ⁵) = dark matter conduit (1/φ³). The observer creates the gold axis by adding one Cantor recursion.
