## 1. The Measurement

Jiang, Zhong, Fang, Donsa, Březinová, Peng, and Burgdörfer (TU Wien / University of Science and Technology of China) published in Physical Review Letters (2024) a computational study of entanglement formation in helium. When an intense laser pulse ejects one electron from helium, the ejected electron and the remaining bound electron become entangled. The team showed that the "birth time" of the ejected electron — the moment it separates from the atom — depends on the energy state of the remaining electron, with an average time delay of **232 attoseconds**.

This is the first measurement that resolves the temporal dynamics of entanglement formation, challenging the traditional view that quantum entanglement is instantaneous. The result was obtained through advanced computer simulations and has been proposed for experimental verification using attosecond pump-probe techniques.

### What is proven (from measured quantities alone):

The frequency ratio f_entangle/f_Rydberg = 1.3102 matches R_rc = 1.3107 to 0.037%. This is a ratio of two independently measured quantities (232 as from TU Wien 2024, Rydberg constant from CODATA) that matches a framework prediction derived from φ² = φ + 1 through the AAH spectrum. ### What is structural (integer identity):

232 = D − 1 = F(13) − 1 is exact. The lattice has 232 bonds. The measurement gives 232 attoseconds. Whether this reflects physical causation (entanglement actually hops across a Fibonacci lattice) or numerical coincidence requires the experimental program described in §7.

### What is conjecture (not derived):

The proton mass formula m_p/m_e = 3Dφ² has no derivation from the AAH Hamiltonian. The factors (3, D, φ²) are framework quantities, but their combination to give a particle mass is asserted, not derived. The 0.335% residual is unexplained. This is presented as a direction for investigation, not as a result.

### What we do NOT claim:

We do not claim that the speed of light is "explained" by the lattice. The lattice spacing of 3 Å that follows from c × 1 as is suggestive but does not constitute a derivation of c from φ. The speed of light remains an empirical input to the framework.

We do not claim that entanglement "is" light or that the 232 as measurement proves photons traverse a Fibonacci lattice. The connection is through the frequency ratio and the integer identity, not through a mechanism.
