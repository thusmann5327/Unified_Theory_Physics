## Abstract

The Aubry-André-Harper (AAH) Hamiltonian at quasiperiodic frequency α = 1/φ and critical coupling V = 2J produces a Cantor-set energy spectrum with 34 gaps whose relative widths are scale-invariant. We show that these 34 gap fractions, applied to any physical scale from the proton to the observable universe, predict structural void sizes that match observations with no free parameters. The two dominant gaps (each 32.4% of the spectral range) correspond to the dark matter walls (σ₂, σ₄) flanking the baryonic center plane (σ₃, band width = 0.04854, matching Planck Ω_b = 0.04860 to 0.12%). Applied to the observable universe diameter (93 Gly), the gap fractions predict void sizes matching the Boötes Void (predicted 254 Mly, observed 250 Mly, 1.6% error), Sloan Great Wall (predicted 1,346 Mly, observed 1,380 Mly, 2.5% error), KBC Void (predicted 2,177 Mly, observed 2,000 Mly, 8.9% error), and Dipole Repeller scale (predicted 588 Mly, observed 600 Mly, 2.0% error). The same fractions applied to the Milky Way diameter predict inter-arm gap widths of 2.5–3.4 kly, matching observed spiral arm spacing. Nuclear physics and cosmology are shown to be manifestations of the same AAH Cantor spectrum at different bracket levels, with the gap pattern serving as a Rosetta Stone between them.
