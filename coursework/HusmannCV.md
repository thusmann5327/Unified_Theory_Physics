## TEACHING PHILOSOPHY

Physics is the art of finding the rules the universe cannot break. My teaching philosophy centers on three convictions:

**Curiosity first.** Every lesson begins with a question students genuinely want answered — a mystery, a paradox, or a real-world phenomenon. Equations emerge as tools to solve those mysteries, not as ends in themselves.

**Depth over speed.** I believe in slowing down to build genuine understanding. A student who truly grasps Newton's Second Law — who can feel it in their bones — will outperform a student who memorized fifty formulas without comprehension.

**Real mathematics.** I do not shy away from the math. I teach students to read and write equations as a language, not a burden. Scaffolded carefully, algebra-based physics is accessible to every 10th grader.

My classroom will be a place where getting the wrong answer is celebrated as a step toward the right one — where students learn that science is not a collection of facts but a living conversation between humanity and nature.
