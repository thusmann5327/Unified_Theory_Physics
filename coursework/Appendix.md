## APPENDIX E — Kinematic Equations (Constant Acceleration) [Y1]

> ⚠️ Valid **only** when acceleration is constant. Use numerical methods for variable acceleration.

| Equation | Variables | Best When... |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| v = v₀ + at | v, v₀, a, t | No displacement needed |
| x = v₀t + ½at² | x, v₀, a, t | No final velocity needed |
| v² = v₀² + 2ax | v, v₀, a, x | Time is unknown |
| x = ½(v₀ + v)t | x, v₀, v, t | Acceleration unknown |

**Symbol reference:** x = displacement (m) · v₀ = initial velocity (m/s) · v = final velocity (m/s) · a = acceleration (m/s²) · t = time (s) · g = 9.8 m/s² downward

### E.1 — Projectile Motion

| Component | Acceleration | Velocity | Position |
|-----------|-------------|----------|---------|
| Horizontal | aₓ = 0 | vₓ = v₀cosθ (constant) | x = v₀cosθ · t |
| Vertical | aᵧ = −g | vᵧ = v₀sinθ − gt | y = v₀sinθ · t − ½gt² |
| At max height | — | vᵧ = 0 → t_peak = v₀sinθ/g | — |
| Resultant speed | — | v = √(vₓ² + vᵧ²) | — |

### E.2 — Tsiolkovsky Rocket Equation [Y2]

**Δv = v_e · ln(m₀/m_f)**

| Symbol | Meaning | Example (Falcon 9) |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| Δv | total velocity budget | ~4,877 m/s (1st stage) |
| v_e = Isp × g | effective exhaust velocity | 311 s × 9.8 = 3,048 m/s |
| m₀ | initial mass | 549,054 kg |
| m_f | final mass (no propellant) | 111,000 kg |

## APPENDIX F — Newton's Laws & Force Formulas [Y1]

| Law / Formula | Equation | Key Notes |
|--------------|----------|----------|
| Newton's 1st Law | ΣF = 0 ↔ a = 0 | Inertia: constant velocity with no net force |
| **Newton's 2nd Law** | **ΣF = ma** | Direction of a = direction of ΣF |
| Newton's 3rd Law | F_AB = −F_BA | Equal & opposite on *different* objects — never cancel |
| Weight | W = mg | g = 9.8 m/s² downward; weight ≠ mass |
| Normal force | N = mgcosθ | On incline angle θ; N = mg on flat surface |
| Kinetic friction | f_k = μ_k N | Opposes sliding; μ_k < μ_s always |
| Static friction | f_s ≤ μ_s N | Maximum just before sliding |
| Hooke's Law | F = −kx | k = spring constant (N/m); restoring force |
| Universal gravitation | F = Gm₁m₂/r² | r = center-to-center distance |
| Centripetal force | F_c = mv²/r | Net inward force; NOT a new force type |

### F.1 — Common Coefficients of Friction
