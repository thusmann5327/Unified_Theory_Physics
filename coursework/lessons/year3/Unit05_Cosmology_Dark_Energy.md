## Day 1-2: The Observable Universe

### Scale of the Universe

```
Observable radius: 46.5 billion light-years (comoving)
Age: 13.8 billion years
Number of galaxies: ~200 billion
Number of stars: ~10²⁴
```

### The Cosmic Microwave Background

- **Discovery:** 1965 (Penzias & Wilson)
- **Temperature:** 2.725 K (uniform to 1 part in 100,000)
- **Origin:** 380,000 years after Big Bang (recombination)
- **Significance:** Oldest light we can see

### Hubble Expansion

```
v = H₀ × d

H₀ ≈ 70 km/s/Mpc (current value)
```

Everything is moving away from everything else.

## Day 3-4: Evidence for Dark Matter

### Galaxy Rotation Curves

**Expectation:** v ∝ 1/√r (Keplerian decline)
**Observation:** v ≈ constant out to large r

**Implication:** There's mass we can't see extending far beyond visible galaxies.

### Gravitational Lensing

Mass bends light (general relativity). Measured lensing exceeds what visible matter can produce.

### Bullet Cluster

Two galaxy clusters collided. The gas (visible) was slowed by collision, but the mass (measured by lensing) passed through. The mass isn't the gas!

### CMB Anisotropies

Acoustic oscillations in early universe require dark matter to seed structure formation.

### Dark Matter Properties

- Doesn't emit/absorb light
- Interacts gravitationally
- Doesn't interact (much) electromagnetically
- ~27% of universe's energy density

## Day 5-6: Evidence for Dark Energy

### Type Ia Supernovae

Standard candles: Same peak luminosity, so apparent brightness gives distance.

**Discovery (1998):** Distant supernovae are dimmer than expected.

**Implication:** Universe expansion is ACCELERATING.

### Dark Energy Properties

- Causes accelerating expansion
- Constant energy density (doesn't dilute with expansion)
- ~68% of universe's energy density
- Acts like negative pressure: P = -ρc²

### The Cosmological Constant

Einstein's Λ term in general relativity:

```
G_μν + Λg_μν = 8πG/c⁴ × T_μν
```

Einstein added then removed it ("biggest blunder"). Now it's back!

## Day 7-8: The ΛCDM Model

### Standard Cosmology

| Component | Symbol | Fraction (Ω) | Nature |
|-----------|--------|--------------|--------|
| Dark energy | Λ | 0.68 | Cosmological constant |
| Dark matter | CDM | 0.27 | Cold dark matter |
| Baryonic matter | b | 0.05 | Ordinary atoms |
| Radiation | r | ~10⁻⁴ | Photons, neutrinos |

### Friedmann Equations

```
(ȧ/a)² = 8πG/3 × ρ - k/a² + Λ/3

ä/a = -4πG/3 × (ρ + 3P/c²) + Λ/3
```

### Success of ΛCDM

- CMB power spectrum (exquisite fit)
- Large-scale structure
- Big Bang nucleosynthesis
- Hubble expansion

### Problems with ΛCDM

- **Cosmological constant problem:** Why so small? (10⁻¹²² of "natural" value)
- **Coincidence problem:** Why are Ω_Λ and Ω_m similar NOW?
- **Dark matter detection:** Decades of searches, no direct detection

### Statistical Significance

```python
# Is the agreement significant?
# Null hypothesis: random coincidence

# If ratios were random (uniform on simplex),
# probability of matching any a + b + c = 1 identity
# to within observed accuracy is...

# Monte Carlo test
np.random.seed(42)
n_trials = 100000
matches = 0

for _ in range(n_trials):
    # Generate random 3-simplex point
    r = np.random.exponential(size=3)
    r /= r.sum()
    r.sort()  # r[0] < r[1] < r[2]

    # Check if matches φ-partition ordering (M < DM < DE)
    # with similar ratios
    if abs(r[2] - DE_phi) < 0.07 and abs(r[1] - DM_phi) < 0.04:
        matches += 1

p_value = matches / n_trials
print(f"Monte Carlo p-value: {p_value:.4f}")
```
