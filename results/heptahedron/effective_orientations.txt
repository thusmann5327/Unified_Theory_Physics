EFFECTIVE ORIENTATIONS: Monte Carlo Heptahedral Tiling
============================================================

Three junction rule sets tested (500 MC runs each):
  LOOSE   — 3 forbidden pairs only (G↔B, G↔BG, G↔BGS)
  STRICT  — 17 allowed pairs only (neutral = forbidden)
  PHYSICS — same-type + shared-letter pairs only (6 blocked)

RESULT: All three rule sets converge to 60.
        Junction matching does NOT reduce 60 → 13.

SCALING (median orientations vs tiling size):
Mode         @20   @50  @100  @200  @300
────────── ───── ───── ───── ───── ─────
loose         17    34    48    58    60
strict        17    34    48    58    60
physics       17    34    49    58    60

NOTABLE COINCIDENCES IN SCALING:
  @20 tiles:  median = 17 = number of ALLOWED junction types
  @50 tiles:  median = 34 = F(9) = number of Cantor gaps in D=233
  @300 tiles: saturates at 60 (all orientations appear)

FORBIDDEN BLOCKING RATES:
  LOOSE:   11.8% of placement attempts blocked
  STRICT:  39.5% of placement attempts blocked
  PHYSICS: 16.2% of placement attempts blocked
  → Higher blocking rate does NOT reduce effective orientation count

JUNCTION DIVERSITY:
  LOOSE  sample: 25 junction types appear (of 25 allowed)
  STRICT sample: 17 junction types appear (of 17 allowed)
  All allowed junction types are realized in every large tiling.

FACE TYPE DISTRIBUTION (200-tile random tiling, STRICT):
  G     16.0% (target  7.4%) — over-represented
  S      7.1% (target  2.9%) — over-represented
  B      5.2% (target  8.1%) — under-represented
  GS    17.4% (target 14.6%) — close
  BG    16.1% (target 29.4%) — under-represented
  BS    16.9% (target 22.6%) — under-represented
  BGS   21.4% (target 14.9%) — over-represented
  → Random tiling does NOT reproduce the target fractions
  → A non-random (quasiperiodic) placement rule is needed

WHY 13 DOES NOT EMERGE FROM FACE MATCHING ALONE:
  The 60 icosahedral rotations produce 60 distinct face-type
  permutations. At each face, there are always multiple allowed
  orientations among the 60 (minimum: 22 for B-face in STRICT).
  Since every face can always be filled, no orientation is ever
  eliminated globally.

  For 60 → 13 to occur, the constraint must be GEOMETRIC:
  - 3D packing: tiles must fit without overlaps
  - Vertex matching: where 3+ tiles meet at a point
  - Quasiperiodic order: aperiodic matching rules (arrows/marks)

  The face-type matching rules constrain WHICH junctions form
  but not WHICH orientations exist.
