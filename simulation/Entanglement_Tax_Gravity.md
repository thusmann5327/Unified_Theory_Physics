## 5. BRACKET PROMOTION — HOW GRAVITY WELLS FILL

### 5.1 The Mechanism

Each bond in the lattice carries an entanglement tax proportional to its depth in the Cantor hierarchy. When a bond's local strain drops below its tax (the gravity well is "full"), the bond's tax does not vanish — it **promotes up one bracket** and donates to the larger well:

1. Bond at bracket level d reaches equilibrium (strain < tax)
2. Its tax × T (one transmission step) promotes to bracket level d+1
3. The parent well deepens by the promoted amount
4. Short-range bonds settle first → medium-range → long-range

This is the identity 1/φ⁴ + 1/φ⁵ = 1/φ³ in action: matter (1/φ⁴) plus one recursion step (1/φ⁵) equals the dark matter conduit (1/φ³). The tax cascades upward through the hierarchy.

### 5.2 The Hierarchy of Settlement

Silver bonds (hard, mass) settle first — they have the highest stiffness and reach equilibrium fastest. Their tax promotes upward, creating the mass hierarchy. Gold bonds (soft, momentum) settle later, maintaining propagation channels while the mass structure builds.

At planetary scales (~bracket 243), the well depth is the accumulated promoted tax from every settled level below — atomic bonds → molecular → geological → planetary. This is real gravitational binding, emergent from the bottom up.

### 5.3 Implications for Planetary Formation

In standard N-body simulations, small objects float off planetary surfaces because gravity is computed as 1/r² between point masses — the end result, not the mechanism. With bracket promotion:

- Atomic-scale bonds settle → tax promotes to molecular scale
- Molecular bonds settle → tax promotes to geological scale
- Geological structures settle → tax promotes to planetary scale
- Result: a deep gravity well built hierarchically from below

Objects placed on a planet's surface encounter a well whose depth is the sum of all promoted tax from every settled sub-scale. They stick because every level below them has already donated its binding energy upward.

### 5.4 Equilibrium Depth and the Matter Fraction

When depth accumulates at rate W (mass/decay per bracket) and reaches equilibrium against a unit drain, the steady-state depth is:

$$d_{\text{eq}} = \frac{W}{1 + W} = 0.3184$$

**Match: 1.2%.** The equilibrium well depth IS the matter fraction. A universe that has fully settled — all brackets promoted to equilibrium — has exactly the matter content we observe.
